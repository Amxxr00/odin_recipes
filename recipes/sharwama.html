ALTE SHARWAMA

DESCRIPTION

A shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish known for its flavorful and savory qualities.

INGREDIENTS AND PREPARATION

Shawarma begins with marinated meat, traditionally either chicken, lamb, beef, or a combination thereof. The meat is thinly sliced and stacked in a cone-like shape on a vertical rotisserie. The marinade typically includes a mix of spices such as cumin, paprika, turmeric, garlic, and sometimes yogurt or lemon juice to tenderize and flavor the meat.

COOKING PROCESS

As the rotisserie turns, the outer layer of the meat slowly cooks and crisps up. This slow cooking process allows the meat to retain its juices and flavors while developing a delicious charred exterior.

SERVING

  1. Flatbread: The most common type of flatbread used is pita bread or a similar Middle Eastern flatbread like laffa or taboon. The bread is often warmed or lightly toasted before assembling the shawarma.
  2. Meat: Thin slices of roasted meat (chicken, beef, lamb, or a combination) are placed inside the flatbread. The meat is usually juicy and flavorful from the marinade and slow-roasting process.
  3. Vegetables: Fresh vegetables are added for crunch and freshness. Common choices include shredded lettuce, slices of tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions. Sometimes pickles or pickled vegetables are also included.
  4. Sauces: Shawarma is often topped with a variety of sauces or spreads, which can vary depending on regional preferences. Popular choices include tahini (sesame seed paste) sauce, garlic sauce, hummus, yogurt-based sauces, or spicy sauces like harissa.
  5. Additional Flavors: Some variations might include additional ingredients such as pickled turnips, sumac, parsley, or even French fries inside the wrap for added texture.
  6. Wrapping: Once the ingredients are placed inside the flatbread, it is wrapped or rolled tightly into a cone or cylinder shape, often wrapped in wax paper or foil to hold it together and make it easier to eat.
